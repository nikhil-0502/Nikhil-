BA Economics, Madras School of Economics  ·  MSc Quantitative Finance, LSE (incoming)
I am a final-year undergraduate at the Madras School of Economics, heading to the MSc Quantitative Finance at the London School of Economics. What caught my attention was seeing measure-theoretic ideas, especially risk-neutral expectations, show up in actual pricing formulas rather than staying abstract. Since then, I have been building and testing models around that: simulating GBM paths, pricing options with Monte Carlo, and checking how sensitive outputs are to discretisation, volatility, and parameter shifts. Most of this I have implemented in Python and C++, focusing less on getting a number and more on how stable that number actually is. I am interested in the parts where models stop behaving cleanly, where assumptions start to matter.

Liquidity Regimes as a Markov Chain and Optimal Execution as a Markov Decision Process
Discrete-time Markov chain model of market liquidity regimes combined with a formal MDP framework for optimal trade execution. The optimal policy is state-dependent: execute aggressively in high-liquidity states, conservatively in stressed states.

F · 03 · Mathematical Finance
Financial Contagion and Cross-Market Return Transmission: S&P 500 and Nifty 50
Estimates lagged return spillovers using OLS, logistic regression, and a two-state Markov regime-switching model. The stress-regime coefficient is approximately three times the tranquil-regime coefficient, confirming state-dependent contagion.
