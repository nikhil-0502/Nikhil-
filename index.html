01 — About

Background

I am a final-year undergraduate at the Madras School of Economics in Chennai, where I have maintained a CGPA of 9.66 and been placed on the Dean's List in three consecutive semesters — including a perfect 10.0 in Semester III. My academic formation has been quantitative throughout: alongside the standard economics curriculum, I have taken courses in real analysis, stochastic processes, advanced differential equations, time series analysis, and linear algebra, building the mathematical foundations that my research now draws on directly.

In the coming year, I will be at the London School of Economics reading Quantitative Finance — a programme that covers the mathematics of Black-Scholes, Itô calculus, stochastic differential equations, derivatives pricing and hedging, machine learning in finance, and advanced time series. I expect it to bring my existing work on Markov chains and regime-switching models into contact with the full apparatus of modern quantitative finance.

My research interests sit broadly across mathematical finance and empirical economics. On the finance side, I am drawn to problems in stochastic modelling — credit risk, market microstructure, and optimal execution — with a particular interest in how probabilistic structure can be embedded in economically meaningful frameworks. On the economics side, I am interested in welfare analysis and development economics, especially where the two fields can be connected through dynamic and stochastic methods.

In my own time, I work in C++ and Python — implementing Monte Carlo price paths, implied volatility surfaces, and option sensitivities — and study topics such as measure-theoretic probability and the role of fractional calculus in derivative pricing. I am a FIDE-rated chess player (~1500), have represented Chennai District and Tamil Nadu State, and play keyboard. I volunteer regularly.

Credit Rating Transitions and Risk-Neutral Pricing of Defaultable Bonds

A finite-state Markov chain model of credit migration with an absorbing default state. Derives the transition probability matrix, multi-period survival probabilities, and risk-neutral bond prices linked to first-passage default probabilities and recovery rates.

F·03

Financial Contagion and Cross-Market Return Transmission: S&P 500 and Nifty 50

Estimates lagged return spillovers between the S&P 500 and Nifty 50 using OLS, logistic regression, and a two-state Markov regime-switching model. Identifies a statistically significant transmission coefficient (β ≈ 0.28) and demonstrates that contagion intensifies under high-volatility regimes.
